# MIRF Currents Constitution (Phase 0 Baseline)

1) Preamble
- Purpose: Establish the foundational rules governing MIRF Currents as a representative, wallet-connected parliament with transparent ballots, auditable logs, and a lightweight executive layer.
- Scope: Applies to Phase 0 governance and gates the transition to Phase 1 and beyond.
- Core principles: Transparency, Representation, Integrity, Innovation, Impact.

2) Mission, Vision, and Values (Public)
- Mission: To establish transparent, representative governance for global impact funding through blockchain technology and community-driven decision-making.
- Vision: A world where governance reflects the people it serves with complete transparency and accountability.
- Values: Transparency, Representation, Integrity, Innovation, Impact

3) Definitions
- Stakeholder: Any entity with an interest in MIRF Currents and eligible to participate in governance.
- Candidate: Individual seeking a seat in the Parliament.
- Seat: A parliamentary position held by a candidate.
- Parliament: The collective body of elected parliamentarians.
- Head of Parliament: The top executive role; direction and tie-break authority.
- Vice Head: Deputy executive; presides in Head’s absence.
- Secretary: Keeps minutes, publishes ballots and outcomes, ensures transparency.
- Parliamentarian: An elected member of Parliament.
- Quorum: Minimum number of seats required to validate a decision.
- NFT Seat (pilot): Tokenized governance seat as a pilot program.
- Ballot: A formal voting process for proposals or elections.

4) Membership & Entry
- Entry eligibility: Candidates must meet baseline participation and conduct criteria.
- Seat entry model (Phase 0): 15 seats in Phase 0; scalable in later phases.
- Open enrollment window and cap mechanics
- Guardrails to prevent manipulation/capture

5) Structure & Seats
- Assembly size (Phase 0): 15 seats.
- Rotation and terms: 24-month terms; staggered elections (1/3 seats up for election every 8 months).
- Regional representation: If used, map regions to seats; otherwise operate as a representative body without hard quotas.

6) Elections & Voting Rules (Phase 0)
- Nomination process: Off-chain nominations with community deliberation.
- Ballot: On-chain verification of results with auditable logs.
- Voting thresholds: Standard Decisions (simple majority >50%), Major Decisions (supermajority >66%), Emergency Decisions (simple majority + Head approval).
- Quorum: Standard 60% of seats present; Major 75%; Emergency 50% with 48-hour notice.
- Privacy vs transparency: Voter anonymity preserved, voting results published by position, not voter identity.
- Results publication: Outcomes public and verifiable.

7) Executive Roles
- Head of Parliament: Executive direction, final decision tie-breaker, strategic vision; term aligned with Parliament (24 months) or as defined by rules.
- Vice Head: Deputy decision-making; presides in Head’s absence; 24-month term.
- Secretary: Minutes, ballot publication, transparency assurance; 24-month term.
- Selection: Elected by Parliament from among seated parliamentarians for defined terms; tie-breaker rules specified.

8) Committees (Phase 0)
- Governance Committee: Rules, policies, procedures.
- Finance & Treasury Committee: Treasury management, investments.
- Impact & Metrics Committee: KPI definitions, impact verification.
- Partnerships & Outreach Committee: Partner selection, community engagement.

9) Amendments & Evolution
- Amendment path: Proposals, deliberation, parliamentary vote, publication.
- Phase transitions: Clear gates (Phase 0 → Phase 1, etc.) with ratification requirements.
- Versioning: Document versioning with change logs.

10) Dispute Resolution & Ethics
- Internal disputes: Committee review → full Parliament vote → independent arbitration if needed.
- Conflict of interest: Disclosure, recusal, and public recording.
- Code of conduct: Expected behavior and governance ethics.

11) Records, Transparency, & Publication
- Public disclosures: Minutes, ballots, decisions; on-chain or verifiable logs where appropriate.
- Data handling: Public vs private content boundaries; IPFS/hash anchors for verifiable documents.

12) Reference & Annexes
- Linkages to Living ByLaws, Whitepaper, and Architecture maps.
- Glossary of terms and references.

13) Amendment & Versioning log (appendix)
- Version, date, author, summary.

14) Quick Reference (optional)
- Summary bullets for leaders and new participants.

Notes
- This Constitution draft is public. It can be refined as governance practice evolves.
- We can later introduce weighted voting, differing term lengths, or alternative executive powers based on phase feedback.
